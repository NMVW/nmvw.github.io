Elusive, subtle, and unintuitive, recursive thinking is a fundamental pattern that defines much of the world.

The trickiest part to understanding recursion is to figure out how to clearly define the recursive problem.

Base Case

Define it concisely and carefully. The base case signals the completion of our algorithm. Think of it as the quantum unit of your solution. Without it, you'll never find an answer; with it, we have a shot at massaging a solution. Always begin your recursive thinking with a powerful base case in mind to reduce algorithmic complexity.

Recursive Case

The circular logic that drives the algorithm to our outcome, the recursive case unravels our complex problem into increasingly digestible fragments that behave similarly enough to their ancestral fragments to be described with a simple relationship.

Mindset

Recursion is a way of thinking that approaches problem-solving as the pursuit of the minimal symmetrical interface that reflects internally within a larger problem. Once a prime example (pun intended) is discovered, one can leverage that indivisible schema to build a solution for the greater problem. It is a tool for a pragmatist to induct complex non-linear problems with bite-sized bits of logic while hammering on computational resources to coax a solution.

Note: Tail end is the optimal type of algorithm design for recursion because the stack frame can be freed at each inceptive level of the call stack.
