First App Reflection

06 April 2016

Scope

I built my first app. I conceived, designed, built and deployed it within 24 hours.

MVP

I started with something small and simple, a city weather-matching service. The app is freely accessible thanks to Heroku's deployment services and the source code is open to inspire on GitHub.

Lessons

I've finally made it. I am cured of fundamental technical ignorance and have begun to see a world of possibilities.

APIs

When I successfully integrated the first API (OpenWeatherMap) into the project, I grew excited. Although I was careful to maintain my feature development without letting it creep beyond reason, I continued on to integrate a forward geocoding API (OpenCageGeocoding) for expanding my data querying abilities with OpenWeatherMap.

Stack

I decided to assemble the app with AngularJS and ExpressJS. I tasked Express with serving static public assets with a couple of lines of code. I determined that a true backend would be excessive. The meat of the work would be handled by Angular in the implicit View-Controller relationship.

Uncertainty

Piecing together technologies that are new to me has been a fruitful experience. At the conclusion of the MVP, I recognized that I had the ability to rapidly create something meaningful. The brief taste of autonomy has penetrated the edge between my growing knowledge and my ability to wield that knowledge.

Nick Vinson

Nick Vinson. Software Engineer.
