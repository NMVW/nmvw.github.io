Spidey Sense Alias

22 May 2016

For those with whom I worked recently on a web security side project, it is time that I disclose the identity of the Spidey Sense alias. It was me.

I wanted to inspire the team to appreciate the power of cross-scripting, so I focused on injecting the server with a playfully demonstrative script before anyone figured out how to block XSS attacks. The following simple script became the Spidey Sense injection:

    "background-color": "rgb(5,29,92)", 
    "color": "white",  
    "font-family": "Comic Sans MS",

    "border": "dashed white 5px" 
  });

  $('body').fadeIn('slow').prepend('').css({
    "border-radius":"100px",

  });

});

The development of web security is paramount to ensuring that the web grows to be a safe and free space to exist, and although XSS is only one channel through which to compromise a host, in this instance, I sought to reveal the susceptibility of our back-end server to our team internally.

Out of hope for discovering another team member's anonymous XSS creativity, I kept my client open to XSS injection. However, instead of another Marvel character XSS injection, a sinister attack from "Ooga Booga" began to inject blocking scripts into my client html.

Now, if you are interested in the latest issues in digital rights and cyber security, check out the Electronic Frontier Foundation. They have been defending civil liberties in the digital world since 1990.
